# HR-Attrition-Analysis
## Project Overview

This project is to identify why employees choose to leave the company. By analyzing HR data, I built a predictive model to identify which employees are at risk of leaving, allowing the company to take steps to retain them.

## Key Findings

The analysis highlighted clear patterns regarding why employees leave:

- **Role-Specific**: Laboratory Technicians are the group most statistically likely to leave.
- **Burnout**: Excessive overtime and frequent business travel are the strongest indicators that an employee is considering resignation.
- **Loyalty**: Employees with more years of work experience and those with a background in Life Sciences tend to stay longer with the company.
- **Volume vs. Risk**: While the Sales department sees the highest number of departures, the high turnover rate among Lab Technicians hints at a specific issue within that role that needs immediate attention.

## Recommendations for HR

- **Targeted Engagement**: HR should prioritize direct conversations with Lab Technicians and Sales Representatives to better understand their specific concerns and challenges.
- **Workload Management**:  Recommended to implement a review process for employees who have been working overtime for two consecutive weeks or more to help prevent burnout.
